About

Hamilton: The Musical is a groundbreaking Broadway show created by the lengedary Lin Manuel Miranda. This live theatre production premiered Off-Broadway on 17-02-2015 at the Public Theater in New York City. The show was later transferred to Broadway, opening on 06-08-2016, at the Richard Rodgers Theatre.

The musical tells the story of Alexander Hamilton(one of the United States of America's Founding Fathers) with the use of hip-hop and rap. It details his journey from an orphan immagrant to the nation's first Treasury Secretary. His family life, struggles as well as his political debacles are presented in a an extremely entertaining way.

Alexander Hamilton

Known for becoming the first Secretary of the Treasury, Alexander Hamilton (1755 or 1757-1804) was one of the founding fathers of the United States. He was a crucial advisor to George Washington during the Revolutionary War after being born in the Caribbean and subsequently migrating to the American colonies. His marriage to Elizabeth Schuyler in 1780, the daughter of a powerful and affluent New York family, boosted his Federalist network and strengthened his status in elite political circles. Together, they had eight kids, and Eliza constantly supported him during his career. He then strengthened federal authority, established the national bank, and laid the foundation for contemporary American capitalism, all of which contributed to the development of the new country's financial system. In addition, Hamilton co-wrote The Federalist Papers, which promoted ratification of the US Constitution. Although Eliza remained by his side, his 1797 admission of an affair with Maria Reynolds caused a stir and damaged his marriage and political reputation. Following his death, Eliza dedicated her life to preserving his legacy by establishing charitable organisations and making sure that his contributions to the country would never be forgotten. In 1804, he was shot in a duel with Aaron Burr, ending his life.


Key characters in the show


  1. Alexander Hamilton - The ambitious orphan immigrant who writes his way into history.
  2. Aaron Burr - Hamilton's patient rival and eventual killer, who also serves as the narrator.
  3. Eliza Schuyler/Hamilton - Alexander's wife, who ensures his legacy lives on.
  4. Angelica Schuyler - Eliza's witty and intelligent sister.
  5. Peggy Schuyler - The cheerful younger Schuyler sister that meets a quiet, untimely end/li>
  6. George Washington - The nation's first president and leader in the revolution.

  8. John Laurens - Hamilton's close friend and fellow revolutionary.
  9. Hercules Mulligan - Street-smart spy and key contributor to the revolution.
  10. Marquis de Lafayette - Charasmatic French aristocrat who fights alongside Hamilton in the Revolutionary War.
  11. King George III - The comedic villain of the story, representing British authority.
  12. Philip Hamilton - Hamilton's peotic first born son
  13. Maria Reynolds - Hamilton's affair partner
  14. James Reynols- The husband of Hamilton's mistress who extorts him.




The story

The musical follows Alexander Hamilton's life in two acts.

Act I:

  1. Hamilton arrives in New York as a poor orphan immigrant from the Caribbean.
  2. He befriends John Laurens, Hercules Mulligan and Marquis de Lafayette.
  3. He becomesGeorge Washington's right-hand man during the American Revolution.
  4. He marries Eliza Schuyler and develops deep bond with her sister, Angelica.
  5. Hamilton begins to show signs of ambition but also recklessness.
  6. The act ends with America gaining their independence.


Act II:

  1. After the war, Hamilton becomes the first Treasury Secretary. He created America's financial system
  2. He clashes with Thomas Jefferson and James Madison in tense Cabinet Battles.
  3. Hamilton's reputation implodes when he publishes theReynold's Pamphlet -a confession to his affair with Maria Reynolds- making him the subject of a political sex scandal.
  4. His son, Philip, dies in a duel.
  5. In the end, Hamilton is killed in a duel with Aaron Burr.
  6. The show closes with Eliza Hamilton works to preserve his legacy.



The show's impact

The show's use of hip-hop and diverse casting transformed Broadway. Around the world, it influenced historical curricula and school activities. It popularised theatre through Disney+ releases, merch and memes. It also sparked debates concerning historical depiction and authenticity. The show is enjoyed and loved by people all over the world regardless of race, language or religion.




Fun facts!

  1. The show took seven years to write.
  2. Tickets became some of the hardest to get in Broadway history, often reselling for thousands of dollars.
  3. Lin-Manuel Miranda first presented Hamilton as a song at the White House in 2009.
  4. King George III's songs are the only ones in a British pop style, contrasting the American revolutionaries.
  5. “Non-Stop” uses over 20 musical motifs from earlier songs.
